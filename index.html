Bears

  1. Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals.
  2. Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight and hearing.
  3. Their great eyesight allows them to detect when fruits are ripe.

Pigeons

  1. pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  2. pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities
  3. pigeons have excellent hearing ablities

Camels

  1. There are two types of camels: dromedary camels and Bactrian camels.
  2. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
  3. Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

Chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptile that are part of the iguana suborder
  2. Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons
  3. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

pandas

  1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  2. An adult panda can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!
  3. Pandas have carnivorous teeth, but they eat bamboo and fruit.

Cicadas

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  2. They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil.
  3. The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
